The 5 Content Jobs

Every single video you make serves exactly one of these. If you can't name the job before you film, don't film yet.

Job 01 · Reach
Call Out the Pain
Mirror their exact experience back at them with zero solution. Make them feel profoundly seen. End without fixing anything. The goal is: "How does he know exactly how I feel?"
Job 02 · Resonance
Paradigm Shift
Challenge a core belief about why they're stuck. "It's not discipline, it's identity." "You're not missing information, you're missing a container." Reframe the problem at the root level.
Job 03 · Resonance
Synthesizer Drop
Share something you consumed, read, or thought about today. "I just re-read this Frankl passage and here's what it means for a 27-year-old who feels stuck." Walk-and-talk. You are the filter.
Job 04 · Respect
Building in Public
Show the behind-the-scenes of building this brand, this life, this program. Not highlights — the real thing. Decisions, doubts, pivots. Proof of concept via your own becoming.
Job 05 · Conversion
Seed the Offer
Plant awareness of the workbook, challenge, or cohort naturally — never a hard pitch. "I built a free thing for exactly this." Never more than once per 5 days.

Your Unfair Advantage: The Synthesizer Identity

You're not a coach who teaches personal development. You're a synthesizer who spent 10 years at the intersection of Ikigai, Eudaimonia, identity psychology, neuroscience, stoicism, and purpose philosophy — and distilled it so your audience doesn't have to. Frame it that way explicitly: "I've spent 10 years studying this so you don't have to. Here's what I actually learned." The musician origin story is the proof of concept. Use it.

The Non-Negotiable Rules

These are the principles that make this specific — not just more content advice.

1

Raw always beats polished

Walk-and-talk, car, desk mid-thought. No waiting for perfect lighting or a "real" setup. The rawness is the message: you're living this, not performing it.

2

Pain before solution — always

Nobody buys transformation before they feel understood. Spend twice as long naming the experience as you do pointing toward the answer. Mirror first, guide second.

3

The hook is a promise, not a trick

Every hook variation should promise a specific feeling or insight. No clickbait. The best hook is the one that makes your exact ICP stop and say "wait — yes."

4

One idea per video. Hard stop.

If you can't finish "This video is about ___" with a single noun or concept, you're making two videos. Cut one. Keep the cleaner one.

5

Specificity is the differentiator

"A 27-year-old who reads every self-help book and still lays awake at 2am" beats "people who feel stuck." Specificity makes your audience feel individually called out. That's the goal.

6

Content is proof, not pitch

Every video you post is evidence that you live this. The coherence between your life (nomadic, intentional, music → coaching arc) and your content is your most powerful trust signal. Mine it.

Weekly Cadence

Volume with intention. This is sustainable and strategic.

Filming (2x/week)

  • Session 1 (Mon/Tue): 90 min → 3 content videos
  • Session 2 (Thu): 90 min → 3 content videos + 1 BTS episode
  • Total: 6–7 videos ready to schedule
  • Film before you overthink. One take, one idea.

Posting (Daily)

  • 4pm block: 30 min max
  • Post → reply to comments → engage 3–5 ICP accounts
  • Post sequence: Pain → Shift → Synth → BTS → Shift → Pain → Seed
  • BTS once per week minimum, twice maximum

Input → Output Engine (The Synthesizer Model)

Anything you read, watch, listen to, or think on a walk is raw material. Voice note → transcript → Content OS → script. The pipeline: consume → voice note → script → film → post.

  • Finished a book? That's a Synthesizer Drop (Job 03)
  • Had a breakthrough on a walk? That's a Pain callout or Shift (Jobs 01/02)
  • Made a decision about the business? That's BTS (Job 04)
  • Client got a result? That's a Seed (Job 05) + Proof

Building in Public Series

A recurring BTS thread documenting what you're building, why, and what you're learning in real time.

What this series is: A weekly episode that pulls back the curtain on building the Best Self brand. It serves two audiences: your ICP men (who see you living the principles you teach) and creator/builder types (who are drawn to watching someone build something real in public). Both audiences convert — one into coaching clients, one into brand evangelists.


The throughline: "I'm not teaching transformation from a distance. I'm in it." Every BTS episode is proof of concept for the coaching itself. The meta-lesson is always: this is what it looks like to actually live these principles.


Format: Walk-and-talk or desk setup. No editing. Under 90 seconds. Post on same cadence as content — not a separate production. It IS the content.
